About

Less is more work

K-dramas are known for their great plots, plot twists,and original soundtracks. They give viewers a glimpse into Korean culture and address relatable issues in society. With their unique storylines and cultural significance, it's no wonder that K-dramas have become a global phenomenon.

The roots of television in South Korea go back to the mid-1950s, with the first Korean TV series broadcasting in 1962. However,it wasn't until the 1970s that Korean households began buying television sets,and more diverse dramas started getting made. The 1980s saw the rise of modern dramas, and in the 1990s, the single-season format became more popular. Today, web dramas are also widespread.
